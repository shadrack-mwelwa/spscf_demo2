About Us

SHANE PERRY SMITH CHRISTIAN FOUNDATION

The Shane Perry Smith Christian Foundation is a Limited by Guarantee non-profit organization, incorporated in 2006 under the Zambian Companies Registration and Incorporation act. We are committed to providing medical care and rehabilitation services to children with Cerebral Palsy, Autism, Down Syndrome, Attention Deficit / Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) and other disabilities with interventions such as Physiotherapy, Speech Therapy, ABM (Anat Baniel Method) Therapy and Stem Cell Therapy. We also cater for Children in disadvantaged situations.

Our Vision

To enhance the capabilities of Children with Cerebral Palsy, Autism etc to live a dignified, independent and fulfilling life to his/her utmost potential without being discriminated or assumed a burden by the society.

Our Mission

Our mission is to integrate every child with Cerebral Palsy, Austism, to the mainstream society by providing them comprehensive rehabilitation care.

Our Leadership

Christine Smith

Founder, Ceo and Director

is the CEO / Founder, and Director of the Shane Perry Smith Christian Foundation. Christine was a qualified insurance broker who specialized in farming insurance in Zimbabwe, and owned her own brokerage, where, she had a client base of 250 top commercial farmers, and 300 commercial clients.

Christine has a University Certificate with the University of Pretoria, in Basic Theology in Contextual Ministry, where she graduated with distinction, and she has graduated from Rhema Bible College, Lusaka, Zambia. Christine has completed various leadership courses, and counselling courses.

Christine leads the Foundation with the vision she believes God has placed in her heart. She has a passion for humanitarian work, and loves to help and see people who had no opportunity in life, to be given an equal opportunity to others. Christine is a widow, and her deep passion is for widows and orphans, and children with special needs. She is a driving force behind work of the foundation.

Miyoba Hamuhuma

Director

Miyoba Hamuhuma, who being handicapped himself, has a passion for working with persons with disabilities and the vulnerable. Miyoba was previously the Executive Director for Cheshire Homes Society in Lusaka.

He has since formed his own NGO called Enlight Abilities which concentrates mainly on blind students, children with physical disabilities, Mother's of children with disabilities, capacity building of leaders of the Disabled People's Organizations and encouraging them to get an education. He has extensive knowledge in Project Management, exceptional community mobilization skills and counseling experience. Miyoba has a Master's in Business Administration and a Bachelor of Arts in Social Work.

Additionally, Miyoba is the US State Department Alumni of the International Visitors Leadership Program (IVLP). Miyoba is also a recipient of an award from the then Vatican Ambassador to Zambia 2012.
