OUR PROGRAMS

Shane Perry Smith Christian Foundation is identifying children with cerebral palsy from all socio-economic backgrounds irrespective of cultural, financial and geographical barriers. We want to increase awareness in society whereby the children with Cerebral Palsy and other challenges are recognised early and are being referred to treatment centres. The Foundation guides the parents of such children for proper treatment using latest treatment modalities available.

What is Cerebral Palsy

Cerebral palsy is a group of neuromotor problems that is characterised by difficulty in ambulation & often accompanied with problem in sensation, cognition, perception, communication, vision, speech, hearing, immunity, epilepsy, feeding, growth etc.

Facts About Cerebral Palsy

It is one of the common causes of severe physical disability in childhood. It occurs because of “brain insult” during early phase of life from foetal life to 2.5 years of age. Problems like severe jaundice, intrauterine infection, trauma, genetic problem, hypoxia and many unknown causes can cause insult to immature brain during early age of life.

Low birth weight & premature born babies are more prone to cerebral palsy. These children are unable to achieve “developmental milestones” as other normal children. These children can be identified at early age by identification of the above-mentioned history, delayed milestone, abnormal social smile, abnormal reflexes, abnormal tone, abnormal movement, abnormal growth pattern and delay in ambulation.

Brain insult is static but, their disabilities can increase, decrease or remain static depending upon environment and treatment intervention.

With early intervention by multimodal therapeutic approach, more than 80% children can have good quality of life and more than 60% can have ambulatory life. Mental retardation is found in only 40% cases.

These children need medical care by a team of medical and rehab specialists which include physiotherapists, occupational therapists, speech therapists, special educators, an orthopaedic surgeon, a neurologist, a paediatrician etc.


Cerebral palsy can't be cured but we can decrease the disability to an extent that most of them can become self-dependent and can be integrated in main stream schooling & society. They can have a professional degree, can have a job, marry and live a long life.

Worldwide vs Zambian Scenario

According to Stanley (2000) Cerebral palsy (CP) is the most common congenital neurologic disorder. The world-wide prevalence of cerebral palsy is 2-2.5/1000 live births.

Worldwide more than 15 million people are affected with cerebral palsy. In developed countries, advanced facilities like institutional care, proper documentation, gait labs, social security, inclusive education, advanced rehab modalities are available. In the last two decades many new rehabilitation techniques have been discovered which has resulted in better living standards of these children.

The Zambian Scenario

Many Children diagnosed with Cerebral Palsy end up dying in early stages of life. They are sometimes considered to be a burden and curse to their families. They are stigmatized. Many educational institutions shun them as they do not have facilities for them, denying them their right to basic education.

These children are not treated well in most health facilities. Parents are left to be frustrated with these children. It looks like society has no place for them and many parents do not see a bright future.

Meanwhile these children, given the necessary help, can not only, reach their full potential but also have an impact in society and the world. It must be noted that children with Cerebral Palsy are most of the time intellectually able to learn anything. They are able to think abstract and early interventions could help them have a relatively better life.

Levels of Prevalence and Access to Treatment

There is over 3000 children living with Cerebral Palsy in Zambia. Despite the high prevelance of this condition in Zambia, there are no medical facilities to offer rehabilitation services to children with Cerebral Palsy. Compounding this already sad state of affairs is the high price of Medication for children with Cerebral Palsy. Especially for the children who suffer from seizures.

Multiple hurdles remain in successful treatment of Cerebral Palsy children in Zambia like:

Lack of clear-cut guidelines for treatment
It is difficult to find proper collaboration from few trained personnel and the Ministry of Health.
Costly Treatment
Requirement of multiple dedicated personnel and Long period of rehabilitation
Training Centers
Lack of training centers for therapists and specialists in advanced modalities of rehabilitation. Combined with low research activity
Poor Treatment Options
Presence of very few specialized centers, Late presentation for treatment and Inadequate facilities for life time care
Lack of Government Support
There are minimal government supportive programs, no government policies for these children in terms of education and employment.

Poor Outcomes for Parent and Child

Parents of such children often wander from place to place in search of a suitable treatment. Some resort to traditional therapy which is in fact harmful for such children. They face many difficulties because of lack of standardized treatment regiment, non availability of advanced and multimodal rehab measures.

Difficulty in getting consultation from various specialty fields of medicine in a single place, difficulty in staying away from their home at rehab centre for long term treatment and no knowledge about future directions and prognosis of the disorder.

Almost all of them face financial problems in the long run. Many face psychological problems, and without clear medical advice they stop treatment all together.

Aim of This Program & Our Strategies For Intervention

This program aims to raise awareness, introduce early intervention measures and care facilities for children with Special needs such as Cerebral Palsy, Autism, Down syndrome, ASD, ADHD etc.

We plan to achieve this by running:

Awareness Programs
To create awareness about comprehensive rehabilitation and early diagnosis of cerebral palsy through awareness camps and educational programs.
Conferences and Workshops
To organize events like conferences, workshops, seminars for awareness and training to all concerned like therapist, social workers, parents etc.
Training of Physicians
Training of physiotherapists, speech therapists, special educators and orthotic technicians specifically for cerebral palsy, Autism etc.
Training of Parents
Training & motivating parents in therapy program for their own child and solving the problem of activities of daily living.
Treatment for Children from Low Income Families
To provide treatment to children from poorest class society with minimal & affordable expenses.
Orthotic Workshops
Conducting Orthotics workshop for development of orthotics and assistive devices to cater specific needs.
Awarenes Programs for Care Givers
To create awareness among children with Cerebral Palsy care takers, general public and media about the recent advancements in the management of various Orthopedic and Neurological disorders.

Offering Rehabilitaion and Care Services

Rehabilitaion Center
Development of a rehab centre having advanced equipment, gait lab and facilities for advanced therapeutic techniques.
Training and Research Institute
Development of advanced institution for training and research in the field of physical disability.
Recreation Center
Development of inclusive education school and recreation centre for children with special needs.
Temporary Home
Development of a Cerebral Palsy, Autism, etc home for temporary stay arrangement of parents along with their children.

We have so far identified 135 children that need to be taken for specialised treatment in India. Help us brighten the futures of these children.

Our Vision

To enhance the capabilities of Children with Cerebral Palsy, Autism etc to live a dignified, independent and fulfilling life to his/her utmost potential without being discriminated or assumed a burden by the society.
